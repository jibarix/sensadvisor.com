ARXIV PRE-PRINT arXiv:2511.19469

Big Wins, Small Net Gains: Direct and Spillover Effects of First Industry Entries in Puerto Rico

Using staggered difference-in-differences on a decade of quarterly municipality data, this study finds a municipality's first sizable industry entries deliver large, persistent local employment and wage gains — but only small net gains once neighboring-area spillovers are netted out.

Short-Run Multi-Outcome Effects of Nightlife Regulation in San Juan

A multi-outcome synthetic-control evaluation of San Juan's late-night alcohol ordinance finds meaningful economic reallocation across restaurants, bars, and hospitality — but no clear change in public-disorder or violent-crime measures.

Disaster, Capital, and Neighborhood Change: Tract-Level Evidence on Post-María Gentrification in Puerto Rico

Tract-level machine-learning evidence that post-Hurricane María upgrading concentrated selectively in low-income neighborhoods with higher education and lower residential mobility — a path-dependent gentrification, with vulnerability measures to guide anti-displacement policy.

Puerto Rico GO-spread models show strong in-sample fit yet collapse out-of-sample — a forensic audit pins the cause on a short rolling-standardization window that manufactures false predictability.

Diagnosing Financial Fragility in a Complex Economy

Reframes financial fragility as a governance and diagnostic problem rather than a forecasting one — showing how leverage, funding dependence, and crowding accumulate quietly in calm periods, with practical guidance for monitoring and stress-testing.

Regulatory Forbearance and Depositor Stickiness in Puerto Rico's Credit Union System: A Chronicle of Act 220-2015 and the Insolvency-Illiquidity Risk

Chronicles how Puerto Rico's cooperative credit unions weathered the 2013–18 collapse in their government-debt holdings — a legislative accounting override (Act 220-2015) that deferred loss recognition, paired with common-bond depositor loyalty that prevented the deposit flight which would have forced bond liquidation at distressed prices.

About Jorge A. Arroyo

xP&A & Data Consultant · Analytics Engineer & Developer

With over 10 years immersed in finance and analytics, I saw a critical need among local businesses: the need to bridge the gap between complex financial planning and the wealth of operational data often locked away in separate systems. SENS ADVISOR was born from an idea to address this: to empower organizations by integrating these domains through expert Extended Planning & Analysis (xP&A) and full-stack enterprise application development that transforms how businesses manage their financial and operational workflows.

I mainly focus on designing and deploying cloud-based xP&A and Business Intelligence systems using Microsoft Azure, Power BI, and advanced Excel techniques, while also architecting sophisticated full-stack business applications with enterprise-grade security, role-based access control, and real-time data synchronization.

Since 2022, I've pursued AI and LLM research as a purposeful hobby to stay current with rapidly evolving technology and to rigorously test what works versus what doesn't in real business implementations. This is how I maintain a ground-truth understanding of AI's current capabilities and limitations which allows me to provide clients with realistic assessments of AI opportunities, honest guidance on implementation risks, and evidence-based recommendations for efficiency gains.
