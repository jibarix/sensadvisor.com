Fill vs operating range 0% = Control floor (rationing) · 100% = Desborde crest (spillover)

Each bar shows where a reservoir sits between its Control floor (the level at which rationing controls begin, 0%) and its Desborde crest (the spillway, 100%). It is a threshold-relative fill gauge, not percent-of-volume. Bars are colored by the alert band the reservoir is currently in; the most-concerning reservoirs sort to the top.

Storage capacity & sedimentation DRNA/PRASA PIRA 2016 · % of original

Each bar is normalized to a reservoir's original design storage (100%): the solid blue part is the share still usable as of the last bathymetric study, the faded brown part has silted in. Bars are ordered worst-first, so you can compare proportional loss regardless of reservoir size — Puerto Rico's older dams have lost heavily, several down to a third or less of their built capacity, which is why a reservoir can hit rationing levels after only a short dry spell. Hover for the absolute volumes (Mm³). Source: DRNA/PRASA Plan Integral de Recursos de Agua (2016), Tabla 5.5 — a 2015 snapshot, so real losses are now somewhat larger.
